Acquisition Risk

What Acquirers Inherit

Every AI acquisition transfers liability that was not visible during diligence. The moment systems are connected, new interaction pathways emerge — carrying oversight gaps, compliance exposure, and successor liability that standard reps and warranties do not address.

// Successor Liability
You Acquire the Liability Surface — Not Just the Asset

When an AI system is acquired, the acquirer assumes responsibility for its behavior from the date of close. Regulatory exposure, oversight failures, and compliance gaps that existed in the target become the acquirer's liability — regardless of what was disclosed in diligence. The Integration Airlock establishes a documented supervisory boundary from day one, creating defensible evidence that oversight was active before liability transfer was complete.

// Kessler Cascade Risk
Unchecked Interaction Growth Degrades the Entire Stack

The Kessler Cascade describes the failure mode where uncontrolled AI system interactions compound — each unmonitored connection spawning further interaction pathways, until supervisory control becomes structurally impossible. In enterprise AI acquisitions, this cascade can propagate across portfolio infrastructure within weeks of close. CTP's Universal Protocol applies deterministic containment at every interaction edge, preventing cascade growth before it becomes irreversible.

Risk Surface 01
Ghost Asset Exposure

AI systems disclosed during diligence represent only the visible surface. Interaction pathways between acquired systems and host infrastructure emerge post-close — creating behavior and liability that no representation or warranty covers. These undisclosed pathways are the Ghost Asset: real, transferable, and uncontained without a supervisory mediation layer.

Risk Surface 02
Oversight Continuity Gap

Standard integration processes do not preserve supervisory control across the convergence boundary. When acquired AI systems connect to production infrastructure, the oversight mechanisms that applied pre-close are not automatically carried forward. The result is a compliance gap at the moment of maximum liability transfer — the Integration Airlock closes this gap deterministically.

Risk Surface 03
Regulatory Audit Exposure

The Airlock provides an immutable, day-one audit record of human-centric oversight, satisfying Article 14 requirements for high-risk AI systems during the convergence phase.

Risk Surface 04
Indemnification Failure

R&W insurance and seller indemnification do not cover AI interaction pathways that emerge post-close. Once systems are connected, the behavior that produces liability is new — generated by the integration itself, not by either party's prior operation. This exposure is uninsured under standard deal structures and requires an architectural containment solution, not a contractual one.

Supervisory Architecture

Three-State Deterministic Finite Automaton

CTP's supervisory layer operates as a formally specified Three-State DFA — Active, Restricted, and Halted. Each state carries defined transition conditions, intervention rights, and audit obligations. The architecture is deterministic: given any system input, the supervisory response is computable, reproducible, and auditable. This is not a policy layer — it is a control-theoretic enforcement mechanism designed to satisfy EU AI Act Article 14's requirement for effective, traceable human oversight.

Active State
Normal Operational Parameters

All AI system interactions are within defined stability thresholds. The supervisory layer observes, logs, and validates each interaction edge in real time. Oversight records are continuously generated. Human operators retain full intervention authority at all times.

→ Baseline Parameter Alignment → Audit Integrity Maintained → Supervisory Continuity Verified
Restricted State
Triggered Intervention & Mitigation

A supervisory threshold has been crossed. The DFA transitions to Restricted state, automatically throttling interaction throughput and routing flagged pathways for human review before proceeding. No flagged interaction completes without an authorized supervisory decision. The Airlock enforces a mandatory review gate — compliant with Article 14's requirement that human oversight be active, not retrospective.

→ Boundary Variance Detected → Interaction Variance Flagged → Supervisory Review Gate
Halted State
Absolute Containment — System Stop

Critical threshold reached. The DFA transitions to Halted state, invoking the cryptographic actuation deadlock gate. All cross-system interactions through the Integration Airlock are suspended until authorized personnel complete a verified re-engagement sequence. The Halted state cannot be exited by system action alone — it requires a deliberate human override, creating an irrefutable audit event documenting supervisory control at the moment of maximum risk.

CTP's Three-State DFA provides deterministic transition points — Active, Restricted, and Halted — that ensure AI interactions are evaluated before they produce outcomes. Key Questions

Questions frequently raised by diligence teams, advisory firms, and legal counsel evaluating Integration Airlock deployment.

What is successor liability exposure in an AI acquisition?

When an AI company is acquired, the acquirer assumes liability for the target's AI systems from the date of close — including oversight gaps, compliance failures, and interaction pathways that were not visible during diligence. Standard reps and warranties do not cover behavior that emerges from the integration itself. The Integration Airlock establishes a documented supervisory boundary before systems connect, creating a documented record that oversight was active before liability transferred.

What is a Kessler Cascade in the context of enterprise AI?

A Kessler Cascade describes the failure mode where uncontrolled AI interaction growth compounds across system boundaries — each unmonitored connection generating further interaction pathways until supervisory control is structurally impossible. In AI acquisitions, this cascade can propagate across portfolio infrastructure within weeks of close. CTP's Universal Protocol applies the Three-State DFA at every interaction edge, containing cascade growth before it becomes irreversible.

How does the Three-State DFA satisfy EU AI Act Article 14?

Article 14 requires effective human oversight, traceable supervisory actions, and documented intervention capability. CTP's Three-State DFA — Active, Restricted, and Halted — provides deterministic transition logic that enforces oversight at the architectural level. Every state transition, human override, and audit event is recorded in an immutable hash-chained log. Oversight is computable and reproducible, satisfying the Article 14 requirement that it be operative, not aspirational.

When in the deal timeline should the Integration Airlock be activated?

Before systems are connected. Pre-close activation creates the baseline that makes post-close oversight defensible — and establishes that supervisory control was operative before successor liability attached. Activating the Airlock after systems have already converged is significantly more costly and produces a less complete oversight record for regulatory and legal purposes.
